The Computer Misuse Act 1990


Even with legislations such as The Data Protection Act keeping peoples data as safe as possible in the hands of companies, there are still a few issues that can come about as a result of malicious intent. The Computer Misuse Act is there to discourage potential hackers who might want to steal or modify your data with heavy fines and prison sentances of up to ten years.

The main points that The Computer Misuse Act are as follows as well as their respective punishments:

  • Unauthorised access to digital material.
    • Up to six months in prison.
    • Up to £5,000 fine.
  • Unauthorised access to digital material with intent to commit a further crime.
    • Up to five years in prison.
    • An unlimited fine.
  • Unauthorised modification of data.
    • Up to five years in prison.
    • An unlimited fine.
  • Making, supplying or obtaining anything that can be used for computer missuse offences.
    • Up to ten years in prison.
    • An unlimited fine.


These clauses make a range of digital malicious activities illegal such as hacking computer fraud, blackmail and the spreading of viruses.
